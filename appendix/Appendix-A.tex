\chapter{Supplementary FFT analysis of the transient RDT--GV simulations}
\label{app:individual_spectra}

This appendix contains supplementary spectral results for the two transient configurations CC\_450\_7 and CC\_400\_6. Sections~\ref{app:fft-timeseries}--\ref{app:fft-coherence} present the time histories with the FFT window, the phase-folded time series, the window robustness check, the per-signal amplitude comparison, the axial evolution of pulsation amplitudes, and the ring-coherence diagnostic. Section~\ref{app:fft-individual} contains the individual full-panel FFT figures for all seven monitor signals in each configuration.

\section*{A.2\quad Phase-folded time series}
\label{app:fft-phasefold}

The five revolutions within the FFT window are overlaid on a common $0$--$360^{\circ}$ phase axis in Figures~\ref{fig:app_phasefold_450_7} and~\ref{fig:app_phasefold_400_6}. A perfect periodic signal would collapse all five traces onto a single curve. The systematic spread between revolutions indicates energy at frequencies below $f_n$ in addition to the rotor and blade-passing harmonics. The deviation is systematic rather than random, pointing to a flow-field feature whose period does not coincide exactly with the rotor period. This is consistent with slowly evolving recirculation structures and passage-to-passage asymmetry in the annular space between the RDT and the GV row.

\label{app:fft-fnfbpf}

In CC\_450\_7 the $f_n$ component dominates every signal by a ratio of 5:1 or more. \section*{A.6\quad Ring-coherence diagnostic}
\label{app:fft-coherence}

The ring-coherence diagnostic $\mathcal{R}_m(f)$ defined in Eq.~\eqref{eq:coherence} quantifies the fraction of the local pressure pulsation that is coherent across the full circumference, i.e.\ the axisymmetric $m = 0$ contribution. It is evaluated by comparing the local wall-pressure signals $P_3$ and $P_5$ (which contain all circumferential modes) with the plane-averaged signal $P_{\mathrm{out}}$ (which retains only the $m = 0$ mode by mass-flow averaging).

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\begin{subfigure}{0.85\linewidth}
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{Figures/FFT/450_7/10_coherence_check.png}
\caption{CC\_450\_7 ($Z_r = Z_s = 7$)}
\end{subfigure}

\vspace{0.5cm}

\begin{subfigure}{0.85\linewidth}
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{Figures/FFT/400_6/10_coherence_check.png}
\caption{CC\_400\_6 ($Z_s = 6$)}
\end{subfigure}
\caption[Ring-coherence diagnostic]{Ring-coherence diagnostic for both configurations. Upper panels: amplitude spectra of the local wall-pressure signals $P_3$ and $P_5$ compared with the plane-averaged $P_{\mathrm{out}}$. Lower panels: amplitudes at the characteristic frequencies as bar charts.}
\label{fig:app_coherence}
\end{figure}

For CC\_450\_7 the diagnostic at $f_{\mathrm{BPF}}$ gives
\begin{align}
    A_{\mathrm{loc}}(f_{\mathrm{BPF}})
      &\approx 26~\mathrm{Pa}
        \qquad (\text{average of } P_3, P_5), \\
    A_{\mathrm{ring}}(f_{\mathrm{BPF}})
      &\approx 20~\mathrm{Pa}, \\
    \mathcal{R}_m(f_{\mathrm{BPF}})
      &\approx 0.75.
\end{align}
For CC\_400\_6 the same diagnostic gives $A_{\mathrm{loc}} \approx 28$~Pa, $A_{\mathrm{ring}} \approx 34$~Pa, and a ratio close to or above unity. In both cases the absolute amplitudes are well below the RSI concern level, so the quantitative value of the diagnostic is less informative than the spectral pattern. The non-negligible $m = 0$ contribution in CC\_450\_7 is qualitatively consistent with the Tyler--Sofrin prediction that $m_{\min} = 0$ for $Z_r = Z_s$. For CC\_400\_6 the local-vs-plane relationship is similar, but the absolute pulsation level is too low to draw a sharp conclusion. The coherence results are consistent with the physical interpretation discussed in Section~\ref{sec:results-fft-physicalexpl}: the non-uniform inflow from the large axial gap suppresses the formation of a clean $m = 0$ breathing mode even in the equal-blade-count case.

The following caveats apply to the interpretation of all results in this appendix. First, no modal analysis of the guide vane has been performed. The conclusion that the cyclic force is below a fatigue-critical level holds only under the assumption that the lowest wet natural frequency of the GV is well separated from $f_{\mathrm{BPF}} = 75.83$~Hz and its low harmonics. A finite-element modal analysis including added-mass effects is recommended as a separate verification step. Second, both simulations were run at the design point $n = 650$~rpm. Off-design operation with stronger swirl or local blade stall could produce qualitatively different pulsation behaviour. The conclusions of this appendix apply to nominal operation only.
